Preston Mayor suggests Napoleon Dynamite Museum for Tourism

Preston, a small town located in southern Idaho, has a potentially-new source of tourism-revenue: A Napoleon Dynamite Museum. Preston Mayor Jim Fiddlesticks proposed the idea during yesterday’s City Council meeting at the Preston Community Center’s main auditorium.

In order to increase the town’s tourism revenue, a 2,000-square foot museum could bring in visitors from across the region. The movie Napoleon Dynamite has garnered cult-like movie fan status among Idahoans and Utahns, and it is in relatively close proximity to major Utah and Idaho cities and towns.

“The Preston Napoleon Dynamite Museum is a sure-fire way to create a family-oriented, fun-filled tourism attraction for the city and all of southern Idaho,” Mayor Fiddlesticks said. “It is a relatively low-cost project that could reap huge benefits for Preston’s education and infrastructure budgets.”

Mayor Fiddlesticks proposed a budget of $125,000 to renovate the unused commercial building at the northwest intersection of Main Street and Potato Road. The budget will be taken out of the city’s surplus funds, which received a boost when an anonymous philanthropist sent a check for the same amount to Mayor Fiddlesticks. The projected tourism revenue proposed by the mayor ranged between $200,000 to $750,000 per city fiscal year.

The City Council meeting was held in the main auditorium because of a freak accident. Preston’s City Hall is currently under repair after lightning struck its historic clock, knocking out power and singing the clock. It was a natural occurrence that would have made Back to the Future fans, Marty McFly, and Doc Brown proud.
