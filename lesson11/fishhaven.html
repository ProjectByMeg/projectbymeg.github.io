Fish Haven declared the Fishmonger Capital of Idaho

Fish Haven, a small unincorporated community in southeastern Idaho, was declared the Fishmonger’s Capital of the State of Idaho by the Idaho Fishmonger Association. The association, comprised of fishmongers across the Gem State, announces the state’s annual Fishmonger Capital each year based on fish sales figures.

“We are proud to announce that Fish Haven won the 142nd Fishmonger Capital of Idaho Award this year,” said Idaho Fishmonger Association President John Pike. Pike’s family claimed that they used to own the famous Pike Place Market in Seattle, Washington.

Fish Haven will receive a trophy and a $500 prize. The trophy will be rotated between prominent community members’ homes throughout the coming year and the prize money will go towards a local charity of the community leader’s choice.

A fishmonger is a person or store which sells fish for food, making it a rare profession in Idaho, which does not have oceans to draw fish from.
