Soda Springs Announces New Geyser Formation

Soda Springs, home of the only captive geyser in the world, announced that it found another geyser formation on the eastern side of the town. The announcement is expected to bring more tourism revenue to the small Idaho town.

“We are proud to announce that Soda Springs’ geyser has a sister on the eastern side of the town. It was an unexpected surprise, but we look forward to preserving the new geyser and developing it into an interactive, family-oriented, and fun-filled experience for residents and visitors,” Soda Springs Mayor Tom Mater said.

Based on several anonymous interviews of town residents, an unidentified man was digging for gold and exclaimed “Eureka!” He thought that the pressure build-up underneath the earth was an undiscovered air pocket where a rumored pirate’s treasure chest was buried. However, much to the man’s surprise, it was a geyser. Residents said that it was incredible that the man escaped harm upon the geyser’s first eruption, though he lost his ten-gallon hat in the process.

The original geyser was discovered in an attempt to find a hot water source for a swimming pool in 1937. It erupts every hour on the hour and reaches heights of 100 feet year-round.
